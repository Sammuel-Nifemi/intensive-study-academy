National Open University of Nigeria (NOUN): Complete Academic Guide

The National Open University of Nigeria (NOUN) is Nigeria’s foremost open and distance learning institution. This guide provides clear, honest, and structured information to help prospective and current students understand how NOUN works, what to expect academically, and who the university is best suited for.

History and Purpose of NOUN

NOUN was established by the Federal Government of Nigeria to expand access to tertiary education through open and distance learning. Its core mission is to remove traditional barriers such as age, location, work commitments, and rigid admission structures.

Unlike conventional universities, NOUN was designed to support lifelong learning and provide flexible academic opportunities for working adults, professionals, and individuals seeking alternative pathways to higher education.

How NOUN’s Academic System Works

NOUN operates an open and distance learning system that combines self-study, online resources, and physical study centres across Nigeria. Students are required to attend the online lectures and obey the Management's guidelines.

Learning is supported through course materials, online platforms, Tutor-Marked Assignments (TMAs), and scheduled examinations.

How Many Semesters Does It Take to Study at NOUN?

For undergraduate programmes at NOUN, the minimum approved duration of study is four (4) academic years.

While the university operates a flexible academic system, completion of any programme is subject to the minimum duration approved for the programme. Delays beyond the minimum period may occur due to factors such as, financial constraints, health considerations, or other unforeseen circumstances.

NOUN’s flexibility allows students to progress at a pace that aligns with their personal and professional commitments.

Direct Entry candidates may have exemptions for certain courses based on prior qualifications; however, overall progression and graduation remain subject to the university’s academic regulations.

What Are TMAs in NOUN?

Tutor-Marked Assignments (TMAs) are continuous assessments conducted entirely online and designed to support students’ understanding of course materials.

TMAs can be completed at the student’s convenience within the stipulated academic timeline, including periods close to or during examination sessions.

However, failure to complete the required TMAs may result in incomplete results or academic penalties, as TMAs form an essential component of the final course assessment.

How NOUN School Fees Work

NOUN operates a structured tuition system in which fees are paid on a semester-by-semester basis. Tuition fees vary across programmes and departments, with different academic disciplines attracting different fee levels.

New students are advised to budget a minimum of ₦100,000 for their initial academic session, depending on programme requirements. In subsequent semesters, tuition costs typically reduce by approximately 20–30 percent, subject to course registration and academic level.

Students make payments each semester in line with the university’s approved fee schedule.

Does NOUN Serve NYSC?

Graduates of NOUN do not participate in the National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) programme. Instead, they are issued an official Exclusion Letter by NYSC.

The Exclusion Letter serves as evidence of NYSC clearance and is widely accepted for employment, postgraduate studies, and other official purposes.

Does NOUN Go on Strike?

NOUN does not operate under the same union-based academic structure as conventional universities. As a result, it is generally not affected by strikes that disrupt academic calendars in other public universities.

This stability allows students to plan their academic progress with greater predictability.

Can I Work and Study at NOUN?

Yes. One of NOUN’s primary objectives is to support individuals who wish to combine education with work, business, or family responsibilities.

However, success at NOUN requires self-discipline, time management, and commitment to independent study.

University Leadership and Administration

NOUN is administered by a Vice-Chancellor and supported by academic and administrative staff across its headquarters and study centres. The university operates under national education regulations and quality assurance frameworks.

Who Should Consider Studying at NOUN?

NOUN is well-suited for working professionals, entrepreneurs, parents, and individuals seeking flexible access to higher education.

Students who prefer structured classroom environments or require constant supervision may need to adjust to NOUN’s independent learning model.
