Do You Need JAMB or UTME to Gain Admission into NOUN?

One of the most common questions prospective students ask is whether they need to sit for JAMB or UTME to gain admission into NOUN.

The simple answer is no. However, an important clarification every applicant must understand is JAMB regularization.

No UTME, No JAMB Exam

NOUN does not require UTME or any JAMB examination.

JAMB Regularization Explained

JAMB regularization is compulsory but involves no exam. It only requires creating a JAMB profile at an accredited centre.

Why It Matters

Regularization validates admission, supports NYSC processing, and prevents graduation issues.

Understanding NOUN Courses and the New Liquefied System

Many students struggle academically not because they are incapable, but because they do not understand the new portal structure.

How It Worked Before

Previously, failed courses could be combined with current courses if unit limits allowed.

How It Works Now

Courses are semester-linked. Failing a prerequisite blocks the next course.

Why Students Must Be Careful

Pending courses can delay graduation and increase costs. Proper planning is now essential.

About the National Open University of Nigeria (NOUN)

Published: 08 January 2026 · Intensive Study Academy

The National Open University of Nigeria (NOUN) is Nigeria’s foremost distance learning university, established to provide flexible and accessible education to learners across the country.

NOUN operates through a well-structured academic calendar, approved course materials, and a centralized examination system designed to ensure academic integrity and uniformity nationwide.

Key Features of NOUN

  • Open and distance learning model
  • Approved courseware and study guides
  • Computer-Based Examinations (CBT) For 100-200L Students and for all GST courses
  • POP Exam for 300-400L Students
  • Nationwide study centres

Students are expected to rely strictly on official materials and institutional guidelines when preparing for examinations.

Understanding GST Courses at 100 Level

Published: 05 January 2026 · Intensive Study Academy

General Studies (GST) courses are compulsory for all NOUN students, especially at the 100 level. These courses are designed to build foundational academic and communication skills.

Courses such as GST101 and GST105 focus on language proficiency, scientific reasoning, and critical thinking required for higher-level studies.

Recommended Study Approach

  • Study approved GST courseware thoroughly
  • Practice with mock examinations
  • Revise summaries before CBT exams
  • Manage time effectively during exams
